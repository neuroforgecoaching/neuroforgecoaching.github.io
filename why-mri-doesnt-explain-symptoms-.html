Why Your MRI Doesn't Always Explain Your Symptoms

MRI findings can be helpful—but they don’t always explain why you feel pain.

One of the most common sources of confusion for people with pain is imaging results that seem to “explain everything.”

But research shows that many structural findings on MRI are also present in people without any pain at all.

1. MRI Shows Structure, Not Pain

An MRI can show discs, joints, ligaments, and tissues—but it cannot show pain.

Pain is produced by the nervous system, not directly by imaging findings.

2. “Abnormal” Findings Are Often Normal With Age

Disc bulges, degeneration, and arthritis-like changes are extremely common in people with no symptoms.

These findings are often part of normal biological change over time, not necessarily a cause of pain.

3. Correlation Does Not Equal Causation

Just because something appears on imaging near the area of pain does not mean it is causing the pain.

This is one of the biggest misunderstandings in musculoskeletal care.

4. The Brain Still Has the Final Say

The brain interprets all incoming signals and decides whether to produce pain based on perceived threat.

This means two people with the same MRI findings can have completely different experiences.

5. Imaging Can Increase Fear

When MRI results are presented without context, they can lead to fear, avoidance, and overprotection.

This can actually make symptoms persist longer in some cases.

What Actually Matters More Than the MRI

  • How your body responds to movement
  • Your ability to gradually build strength
  • Your confidence with activity
  • How sensitive your nervous system is
  • Your overall load and recovery balance

These factors often matter more for recovery than imaging alone.

Better Understanding = Less Fear

The goal is not to ignore imaging—but to understand it in context.

When you understand what MRI can and cannot tell you, it becomes easier to move forward with confidence instead of worry.
